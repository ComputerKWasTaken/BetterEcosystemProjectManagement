# Publishing and Visibility

> Scenarios can be Private, Unlisted, or Published, controlling who can discover and play them.

## Overview

Visibility settings determine who can access your Scenarios. Choose based on whether you want to share your creation publicly, with specific people, or keep it entirely private.

## Visibility Levels

### Private

**Who Can Access**: Only you

**Use For**:
- Work in progress
- Personal use only
- Testing before publication

**Behavior**:
- Not searchable
- No public link works
- Only visible in your My Stuff

### Unlisted

**Who Can Access**: Anyone with the direct link

**Use For**:
- Sharing with specific people
- Beta testing with select players
- Content you don't want searchable

**Behavior**:
- Not in search results
- Direct link works
- Anyone with link can play

### Published (Public)

**Who Can Access**: Everyone

**Use For**:
- Finished, polished Scenarios
- Community contribution
- Building audience

**Behavior**:
- Appears in search
- Ranked in Trending (if popular)
- Fully public

## Setting Visibility

1. Open Scenario editor
2. Find visibility toggle
3. Toggle "Publish" for public
4. If published, toggle "Unlisted" to hide from search

## Publishing Considerations

### Before Publishing

- Proofread all text
- Test thoroughly
- Check all Story Cards work
- Verify Scripts function
- Remove placeholder/test content

### NSFW Content

If your Scenario contains mature content:
- Toggle "Flagged NSFW"
- Required for compliance
- Hides from safe searches

### Comments

Toggle "Allow Comments" to enable/disable player feedback on published Scenarios.

## Trending System

Published Scenarios can appear in Trending:
- Based on recent play activity
- New plays boost ranking
- Engagement affects position

The exact algorithm isn't public, but quality and popularity matter.

## Finding Published Scenarios

Players find published Scenarios through:
- Search (by title, tags, description)
- Trending lists
- Direct links shared by creators
- Browse categories

## Tags for Discovery

Add relevant tags to help players find your Scenario:
- Genre tags (fantasy, mystery, horror)
- Theme tags (romance, combat, exploration)
- Style tags (casual, hardcore, narrative)

## Updating Published Scenarios

You can edit published Scenarios:
- Changes apply immediately
- Existing Adventures keep their state
- New Adventures use updated version

## Play Statistics

Published Scenarios track:
- Number of plays
- May show engagement metrics
- Helps you understand popularity

## Sharing Unpublished Work

To share without full publication:
1. Set to Published + Unlisted
2. Copy the Scenario URL
3. Share link with specific people

They can play it, but it won't appear in search.
