# Opening and Setup

> The Prompt establishes the Adventure's starting point, setting up characters, setting, and initial situation.

## Overview

The Prompt is the most important part of a Scenario. It becomes the first action of every Adventure started from that Scenario. A well-crafted Prompt establishes the world, introduces the player character, and creates an immediate situation to engage with.

## Prompt Purpose

The Prompt should:
- Establish the setting and atmosphere
- Introduce the player character or situation
- Create immediate engagement or conflict
- Set the tone for the adventure

## Prompt Length

You can make the Prompt very long—as long as fits in context:
- More setup = richer starting point
- Use the available space
- Front-load important world-building

**Why Long Is Good**:
- Establishes more context for the AI
- Reduces need for immediate Story Cards
- Creates immersive opening

## Prompt Structure

### Opening Hook

Start with something engaging:
- An action moment
- A compelling description
- An intriguing situation

### Setting Establishment

Describe the world:
- Where is this?
- When is this?
- What are the rules?

### Character Introduction

Establish the protagonist:
- Who is the player (if known)?
- What's their situation?
- What do they want?

### Immediate Situation

End with something to react to:
- A question or choice
- An arriving threat
- A discovered mystery

## Example Structure

```
[Setting establishment - 1-2 paragraphs]
The kingdom of Larion has been at war for a decade...

[Character/situation - 1-2 paragraphs]  
You are a veteran soldier returning home after the final battle...

[Immediate hook - 1 paragraph]
As you crest the hill overlooking your village, you see smoke rising 
from the direction of your family's farm...
```

## Perspective Considerations

### Second Person (Default)

Most Scenarios use "You":
- "You are a knight..."
- "You walk into the tavern..."

### Third Person

If using Third Person mode:
- "The adventure begins..."
- Player enters name at start

### Open Protagonist

Let the player define themselves:
- Don't specify gender, appearance
- Use placeholders for character details

## Avoiding Information Loss

Content at the very top of a long Prompt may exit context quickly as the adventure progresses. To preserve crucial information:

- Put permanent facts in Plot Essentials
- Create Story Cards for important lore
- Keep the Prompt focused on the immediate opening

## Testing Your Opening

Playtest multiple times:
- Does the AI understand the setup?
- Does the opening engage players?
- Is the tone consistent with your vision?
- Do Story Cards activate appropriately?

## Common Mistakes

### Too Much Backstory

Long historical exposition before anything happens. Put history in Plot Essentials or Story Cards.

### No Immediate Engagement

Pure description with nothing for the player to react to. End with a hook.

### Overspecified Player Character

Detailed appearance/personality that may not match player's vision. Use placeholders for customization.

### Conflicting Information

Prompt says one thing, Story Cards say another. Ensure consistency.
