Naming Proposal

Storybridge

Where the narrative meets the world beyond.
Chapter I

Name Analysis

Story (the narrative, the adventure, the reason AI Dungeon exists) + Bridge (a structure connecting two separate places).

The name speaks the language of the user base. AI Dungeon is, at its heart, about stories. Every scenario, every script, every turn is in service of narrative. Storybridge names the moment when a story reaches beyond itself — when it can touch the real world, sense the environment, call upon external intelligence, and render interactive elements — all while remaining a story.

It is warm where other names are technical. It leads with the why (stories deserve more power) rather than the what (here is a runtime).

Chapter II

Narrative Enhancement

Storybridge positions the system as the place where narrative meets capability. The core message is about enriching stories — giving them access to the real world without breaking the narrative spell.

Storybridge connects AI Dungeon stories to the capabilities they deserve. Through BetterDungeon, a story can sense the real time of day, check the weather outside the player’s window, render a glowing health bar that tracks every battle, or ask a hosted AI model to voice an NPC’s inner thoughts. Storybridge is the reason a story can do all this and still feel like a story.

The metaphor is ancient and universal. A bridge connects two sides of a divide. On one side: the story world, alive with narrative and character. On the other: the real world, full of data, intelligence, and capability. Storybridge spans the gap.

Chapter IV

Target Audience

  • Primary: AI Dungeon players and scenario creators who think of themselves as storytellers first and coders second. Storybridge speaks their language.
  • Secondary: New creators who have never written a script but are drawn to the idea of making their stories richer. “Storybridge” is approachable and non-intimidating.
  • Tertiary: The interactive fiction and narrative gaming community at large. The name transcends AI Dungeon’s specific ecosystem and speaks to anyone who cares about narrative craft.
Chapter V

“Heartbeat”The storybridge:heartbeat card — the pulse that says the bridge is open
“Reaching across”Writing request envelopes to storybridge:out

At 11 characters, “storybridge” ties with Ultrascripts and Dungeonwire for the longest card prefix. The expressiveness of the name may justify the length for this audience.

Chapter VI

Design Direction

  • Tone: Literary, warm, inviting — as this document demonstrates. Think book design, illuminated manuscripts, warm materials.
  • Iconography: An arched bridge silhouette, or an open book with a bridge emerging from the pages. Alternatively: two facing pages connected by an arc.
  • Color: Rich gold and cream on deep leather/parchment backgrounds. Warm, saturated, organic materials.
  • Typography: Classic serif for headings (Merriweather, Playfair Display). Clean sans for supporting text. A literary, curated feel.
Chapter VII

Strengths & Risks

Strengths

Story-firstThe only candidate that leads with the narrative. Speaks directly to AI Dungeon’s identity as a storytelling platform.
ApproachableNon-technical, warm, inviting. Lowers the barrier for non-coders who want to enhance their stories.
Universal metaphorBridges are understood everywhere. The concept needs no explanation.
Unique compound“Storybridge” returns near-zero results. Fully ownable.
Narrative vocabulary“Cross the bridge,” “bridge module,” “stories deserve more” — the derived language is rich.

Risks

Longer prefix11 characters. Same as Dungeonwire and Ultrascripts.
Could sound educational“Storybridge” has a faint association with children’s literacy programs. The dark, rich design language should counteract this.
Less technical gravityMay not convey “serious runtime” to developers. This is a tradeoff: what it gains in accessibility, it loses in engineering credibility.
Partially AI-Dungeon-tied“Story” is central to AI Dungeon but less relevant if the system ever serves non-narrative platforms. Moderate concern.
Chapter VIII

Summary

Storybridge is the storyteller’s name. It says: your story deserves to reach beyond the page — and now it can. The marketing focus is on narrative enrichment, accessibility, and the poetic idea that a bridge can connect imagination to real-world capability. It works best if BetterDungeon’s messaging centers the creative community — the people who write stories, build worlds, and dream in narrative.
