Naming Proposal

Nexus

The connection point for everything.
01 — Analysis

Name Analysis

Nexus — a connection or series of connections linking two or more things; the central and most important point. From Latin nexus, meaning “a binding together.”

The name positions the system as the gravitational center where scripts, modules, and the extension all converge. It does not describe a pipe or a wire — it describes a hub. Everything connects through the Nexus.

02 — Positioning

The Central Hub

Nexus positions the system as the convergence point where all capabilities meet. The core message is about centrality — one connected hub that links scripts to every capability BetterDungeon offers.

Nexus is BetterDungeon’s central connection point. Every module, every capability, every data flow passes through the Nexus. Scripts reach out; BetterDungeon responds. Nine built-in modules — from weather lookups to hosted AI calls — orbit the same runtime, accessible through the same protocol. One hub. Everything connected.

Think of Nexus as a constellation map. At the center sits the runtime — the core that tracks adventures, dispatches state, writes heartbeats, and manages module lifecycles. Orbiting that center are the modules: Scripture for UI, WebFetch for external data, Clock for time, Provider AI for intelligence, and five more. Every connection runs through the center.

04 — Audience

Target Audience

  • Primary: Scenario creators and developers who think in terms of systems and connections. Nexus appeals to people who like knowing how things fit together.
  • Secondary: New BetterDungeon users encountering the module system for the first time. “Nexus” immediately communicates that this is a hub of capabilities, not a single feature.
  • Tertiary: The broader gaming and modding community. “Nexus” is widely recognized in gaming contexts (NexusMods, etc.), which gives it instant cultural familiarity — though this is also a naming risk.

06 — Visual

Design Direction

  • Tone: Cosmic, connected, expansive — as demonstrated by this document. Star-field backgrounds, orbital diagrams, network graphs.
  • Iconography: A central node with radiating connections. A simplified constellation pattern. A bright point with orbital rings.
  • Color: Deep indigo and violet with bright white accent points. Cosmic palette that suggests depth and connection.
  • Typography: Modern, rounded sans-serif like Manrope. Approachable but precise. “NEXUS” in all-caps is compact and symmetrical.
07 — Assessment

Strengths & Risks

Strengths

Shortest prefix5 characters. nexus:heartbeat is the most compact card title of all candidates.
Hub metaphorImmediately communicates that this is a central system with many capabilities radiating from it.
Platform-agnosticNot tied to AI Dungeon. Works across any future platform.
Gaming familiarityThe word resonates in gaming culture. It feels like it belongs in the space.
Extensible“Nexus module,” “Nexus-powered,” “connect to the Nexus” — all natural compounds.

Risks

NexusMods associationThe strongest risk. NexusMods is a dominant brand in gaming modding. While there is no trademark conflict in this space, users may initially confuse the reference.
Overused in techGoogle Nexus (phones), Sonatype Nexus (artifacts), various “Nexus” products. The word is popular and somewhat diluted.
Hub implies centralizationCould unintentionally suggest a server-side or cloud component. Messaging needs to clarify this runs entirely client-side in the extension.
08 — Summary

Summary

Nexus is a gravity name. It says: this is the center of the system — the point where scripts, modules, and capabilities all converge. The marketing focus is on connectivity, centrality, and the power of having everything accessible from one hub. It works best if BetterDungeon’s messaging emphasizes the breadth of the module ecosystem and the elegance of having a single connection point to all of it.
